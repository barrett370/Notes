Vector Notation for Documents

Given a set of documents D={d1,d2,…,dN}D= \left\{ d_1, d_2,\ldots,d_N\right\}D={d1​,d2​,…,dN​}, we can consider this a corpus for information retrieval (IR). Suppose the number distinct words in the corpus (DDD) is VVV, this is our vocabulary size.

If we split each document d∈Dd \in Dd∈D into MMM different terms, we can write d={ti(1),ti(2),…,ti(M)}d = \left\{ t_{i(1)},t_{i(2)},\ldots,t_{i(M)} \right\}d={ti(1)​,ti(2)​,…,ti(M)​}. Finally, we assign a frequency to each term s.t. term ti(m)t_{i(m)}ti(m)​ occurs fi(m)f_{i(m)}fi(m)​ times.

With this, the vector representation, vec(d)=d⃗\text{vec}(d) = \vec{d}vec(d)=d of ddd is the VVV dimensional vector:

(0,…,0,wi(1),d,0,…,0,wi(2),d,0,…,0,wi(M),d,0,…,0)\left( 0,\ldots, 0, w_{i(1),d}, 0, \ldots, 0, w_{i(2),d}, 0, \ldots, 0, w_{i(M),d}, 0, \ldots, 0 \right)(0,…,0,wi(1),d​,0,…,0,wi(2),d​,0,…,0,wi(M),d​,0,…,0)

where:

  • wi(1),dw_{i(1),d}wi(1),d​ is the weighting calculated from term frequency ×\times× the inverse document frequency, i.e. =fi(1),d×IDF(i(1))= f_{i(1),d} \times IDF(i(1))=fi(1),d​×IDF(i(1)) and the 111 denotes the i(1)thi(1)^{th}i(1)th place term.

Latent Semantic Analysis (LSA)

Suppose we have a corpus with a large number of documents, for each document ddd the dimension of the d⃗\vec{d}d is potentially in the thousands.

Suppose one such document contained the words 'sea' and/or 'beach'. Intuitively, often, a document contains 'sea' it will also include 'beach'.

Mathematically, if d⃗\vec{d}d has a non-zero entry in the 'sea' component/ dimension, it will often also have a non-zero entry in the 'beach' component.

Latent Semantic Classes

If it is possible for us to detect such structures as the one seen above, then we can start to discover relationships between words automatically, from data.

The above example can be described as an equivalence set of terms including 'beach' and 'sea' which is about the seaside

Finding such classes is non-trivial and involves some relatively advanced linear algebra; the following is only an outline of which.

Interpretation

The matrices U\bf UU and V\bf VV are orthogonal with real numbered entries.

  • U\bf UU is N×NN \times NN×N where NNN is the number of documents in the corpus.
  • V\bf VV is V×VV\times VV×V where VVV is the vocabulary size
    • They satisfy: UUT=I=UTU,VVT=I=VTV\bf UU^T = I = U^TU, VV^T = I = V^TVUUT=I=UTU,VVT=I=VTV
  • The singular values s1,…,sNs_1, \ldots, s_Ns1​,…,sN​ are positive and satisfy s1≥s2≥…≥sNs_1 \geq s_2 \geq \ldots \geq s_Ns1​≥s2​≥…≥sN​
    • The off-diagonal values of S\bf SS are all 0

The columns of V\bf VV are VVV dimensional unit vectors orthogonal to each other. They form a new orthonormal basis for the document vector space. Each column of V\bf VV is a document vector corresponding to a semantic class, or topic, in the corpus. The importance of the topic vnv_nvn​ is given by the magnitude of sns_nsn​

As vnv_nvn​ is a document vector, its jthj^{th}jth value corresponds to the TF-IDF weight for the jthj^{th}jth term in the vocabulary for the corresponding document or topic. Using this we can work out how important to a topic the jthj^{th}jth term in a document vector is to a topic vnv_nvn​. If vn,jv_{n,j}vn,j​ is large then it plays an important role in the topic.

While V\bf VV describes topics as combinations of terms/ words, U\bf UU describes topics as a combination of documents.

Topic-based representations

The columns of V\bf VV , v1,…,vVv_1, \ldots, v_Vv1​,…,vV​ are an orthonormal basis for the document vector space.

If ddd is a document in the corpus then d⃗⋅vn\vec{d}\cdot v_nd⋅vn​ is the magnitude of the component of d⃗\vec{d}d in the direction of vnv_nvn​, i.e. the component of d⃗\vec{d}d corresponding to topic nnn. Therefore, it follows that:

top(d)=[d⃗⋅v1d⃗⋅v2⋮d⃗⋅vn⋮d⃗⋅vV]top(d) = \begin{bmatrix} \vec{d}\cdot v_1 \\ \vec{d} \cdot v_2 \\ \vdots \\ \vec{d}\cdot v_n \\ \vdots \\ \vec{d}\cdot v_V \end{bmatrix}top(d)=⎣⎢⎢⎢⎢⎢⎢⎢⎢⎢⎢⎡​d⋅v1​d⋅v2​⋮d⋅vn​⋮d⋅vV​​⎦⎥⎥⎥⎥⎥⎥⎥⎥⎥⎥⎤​

is a topic-based representation of ddd in terms of v1,…,vVv_1,\ldots,v_Vv1​,…,vV​

Topic-Based dimension reduction

Since the singular values sns_nsn​ indicate the importance of the topic vnv_nvn​, we can therefore, truncate the vector top(d)top(d)top(d) when sns_nsn​ becomes small or insignificant.

top(d)≈[d⃗⋅v1d⃗⋅v2⋮d⃗⋅vn]=V(n)Tvec(d)top(d) \approx \begin{bmatrix} \vec{d}\cdot v_1 \\ \vec{d}\cdot v_2 \\ \vdots \\ \vec{d}\cdot v_n \end{bmatrix} = \textbf{V}_{(n)}^T vec(d)top(d)≈⎣⎢⎢⎢⎢⎡​d⋅v1​d⋅v2​⋮d⋅vn​​⎦⎥⎥⎥⎥⎤​=V(n)T​vec(d)

where V(n)\bf V_{(n)}V(n)​ is the V×nV\times nV×n matrix comprised of the first nnn columns of V\bf VV
We can now say that top(d)top(d)top(d) is a reduced (n)(n)(n) dimensional vector representation of the document ddd

Topic based word representation

Suppose www is the ithi^{th}ith word or term in the vocabulary.

The one-hot vector h(w)h(w)h(w) is the vector:

h(w)=[0⋮010⋮0]h(w) = \begin{bmatrix} 0 \\ \vdots \\ 0 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ \vdots \\ 0 \end{bmatrix}h(w)=⎣⎢⎢⎢⎢⎢⎢⎢⎢⎢⎢⎢⎡​0⋮010⋮0​⎦⎥⎥⎥⎥⎥⎥⎥⎥⎥⎥⎥⎤​

h(w)h(w)h(w) is essentially the document vector for a document consisting of just the word www

We can use h(w)h(w)h(w) to find the vector top(w)top(w)top(w) that describes www in terms of the topics that it contributes to:

top(w)=[h(w)⋅v1h(w)⋅v2⋮h(w)⋅vn]=V(n)Ttop(w)top(w) = \begin{bmatrix} h(w) \cdot v_1 \\ h(w) \cdot v_2 \\ \vdots \\ h(w) \cdot v_n \\ \end{bmatrix} = \textbf{V}_{(n)}^T top(w)top(w)=⎣⎢⎢⎢⎢⎡​h(w)⋅v1​h(w)⋅v2​⋮h(w)⋅vn​​⎦⎥⎥⎥⎥⎤​=V(n)T​top(w)
